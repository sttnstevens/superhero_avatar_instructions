The Core 4 Framework

The Core 4 prompting framework (from the AI Practitioner: Glean Foundations course) helps you write strong image prompts. Click each card to expand it.

1
Task: What do you want Glean to do?
The action you want the model to take. Click to expand.

This is the action you want Glean to take. A clear task tells Glean you are making a character image, not a logo, chart, or landscape.

"Generate a superhero-style avatar of a person who represents my superpower at work."

"Create a trading-card style character portrait of my work hero self."

Start here. Be specific about what kind of image you want generated.

2
Context: Who are you, and what is your superpower?
Connect the image to your real role and strengths. Click to expand.

Context connects the image to your real role, strengths, and environment. Glean can use your enterprise context to ground your avatar in your AHEAD-related work.

"This person works in Talent Development and instructional design. Their superpower is turning complex ideas into simple, clear learning experiences. They help teams adopt AI in practical, human-centered ways."

Or try asking Glean to look at your work first:

"Search for my scope and what I work on. Then generate a superhero avatar that represents my role and expertise visually."
3
Constraints: What must be true (or not true)?
Set guardrails for style, composition, and what to avoid. Click to expand.

Constraints are the guardrails for your image. They control style, pose, colors, and what to avoid.

Style examples:
"Comic book style, bold outlines, vibrant colors."
"Pixar-style 3D character, soft lighting, expressive face."
"Flat vector illustration, simple shapes, high contrast."
Composition:
"Dynamic action pose, full body. Centered character, simple background, no text. Include teal and gold as accent colors."
Things to avoid:
"No logos or real brand names. No text on the image. Do not show any specific real person."
4
Examples: Optional style inspiration
Give the model a direction without copying exactly. Click to expand.

Examples act like references for vibe and style. You are not copying them exactly, just giving the model a direction. If you are unsure, give 1 or 2 references and see what you like best.

"Think Marvel trading card."
"Anime hero aesthetic."
"Realistic and life-like; Power Rangers-style."
"Retro 90s cartoon like a Saturday morning superhero show."
Full Core 4 example combined:
Task: "Generate a superhero-style avatar of a person who..."
Context: "...works in Talent Development as an instructional designer. Their superpower is noticing and refining every detail so nothing falls through the cracks."
Constraints: "Style: early 2000s retro cartoon, thick outlines, simple shading, bright colors. Dynamic pose. No text on the image."
Examples: "Think early 2000s Saturday-morning superhero cartoon, with a nostalgic, retro feel."
The R3 Approach

Follow this simple three-round approach to build your avatar from a baseline all the way to your ideal vision.

🔵
Round 1
Start Simple
Tap to see example prompt
Get a baseline and see how much your details matter
R1 Example Prompt
"Generate an image of a superhero avatar."
It will probably be generic, and that is expected! This gives you a baseline so you can see how much difference your Core 4 details make in Round 2.
🟢
Round 2
Add Context and Constraints
Tap to see example prompt
Now add your full Core 4 details
R2 Example Prompt
"Generate a comic book-style superhero avatar. The hero's superpower is making complex ideas simple. They hold a glowing book that turns complicated text into clear, colorful infographics. Bold, vibrant colors, dynamic action pose. Background shows swirling knowledge and lightbulbs."
⭐
Round 3
Refine and Iterate
Tap to see example prompt
Nudge toward your ideal without starting over
R3 Example Prompt
"I like the overall concept. Make it feel more like an action figure on a collectible card. Add a subtle AHEAD teal accent in the color scheme. Make the pose more confident and heroic."
You are not starting from zero. You are nudging toward your ideal. Small tweaks yield better results faster than brand-new prompts.
🔄
When to Start Fresh
If your avatar has drifted far from your vision and tweaks are not helping, try opening a new Glean Chat and starting over with a clear Core 4 prompt. A clean chat can be easier than fighting against earlier instructions.
Pro Tips

Use these tips while you write and refine your avatar prompt. The difference between a generic result and a great one is in the details.

🎨
Be Explicit About Style
Say things like "Flat illustration, bold colors," "Photorealistic portrait, soft lighting," or "Comic book style with thick outlines." Do not leave style to chance.
⚡
Show Your Superpower Visually
Instead of "My superpower is organization," try "Surrounded by glowing floating checklists and neatly labeled folders, wearing a cape made of colorful sticky notes."
🎭
Set the Audience and Vibe
"Fun and playful, like a character sticker" versus "Epic and dramatic, like a movie poster." The vibe shapes everything from colors to pose to background.
🚫
Limit Text on the Image
Short labels or a single word can work, but long text is often messy or incorrect. Express ideas visually instead. Let the imagery do the talking.
🔄
Iterate, Do Not Start Over
If you are close, say "Regenerate with a simpler background" or "Change the palette to blues and golds." Small tweaks are faster than brand-new prompts.
🛸
Try Different Styles
Experiment! Classic 2D Disney, Spider-Verse 3D, Anime hero, Studio Ghibli, Cel-shaded game style, Flat vector, Retro 90s cartoon, Watercolor, Low-poly 3D. The options are endless.
🗺
Make Your Power Visible
Think about objects like books, puzzles, bridges, and toolkits. Use colors that feel like you. Include environments matching your work such as workshops, dashboards, libraries, or labs.
🤝
You Are the Creative Director
AI is your co-pilot. Review what it creates and refine until it feels right for you. Have fun. This is a creative way to tell your story visually.
⭐
Don't Forget to Choose Your Superhero Name!
Choose a fun name that fits your superpower. For example: The Clarity Crafter, The Pattern Finder, The Bridge Builder, or The Pixel Guardian.

"Now turn that hero description into a Core 4-style avatar prompt for an image model. Include task, context, constraints, and 1 or 2 style examples."
